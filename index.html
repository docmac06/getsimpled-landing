01 — The problem

Most enterprise training teaches people to pass a module. Not to do the work.

Five reasons today's L&D stack leaves employees under-supported on the things they actually have to do every day.

01

Finishing a module isn't the same as feeling ready.

An LMS tells you whether someone clicked through the slides. It doesn't tell you whether they walked away confident enough to apply the process when a real customer, a real edge case, or a real escalation lands in front of them.

02

Content goes stale fast.

SOPs, policies, workflows, tools and regulations change constantly. Employees are left learning from training that no longer matches how the work is actually done.

03

Generic training wastes people's time.

Employees sit through broad, one-size-fits-all content even though their roles, workflows, markets and responsibilities look nothing alike.

04

Certificates don't build confidence.

A completion record proves attendance. It doesn't help an employee feel prepared the next time a policy edge-case or regulator question lands in their lap.

05

People are being asked to use AI with no real support.

Enterprises roll out AI tools faster than employees can be supported in using them safely, effectively, and within real business context.

07 — Compliance

Give employees the confidence to handle the hard calls.

In regulated industries, the real risk isn't a missing certificate — it's an employee who froze on an edge-case they were never properly prepared for. Simpled turns your policies into realistic decision scenarios so people build the judgment to handle them, and L&D sees where extra coaching would help.

Decision scenario · Refund Policy v4.2

A customer disputes a charge 47 days after purchase and claims the product was defective on arrival. The standard refund window is 30 days. What do you do?

Decline — the 30-day window has passed ✕
Apply the defect-on-arrival exception & escalate for QA log ✓
